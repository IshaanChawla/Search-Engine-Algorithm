<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>From <a href="http://www.stroustrup.com/dne.html">The Design and Evolution of C++</a> - Bjarne Stroustrup - Addison-Wesley (ISBN 0-201-54330-3) - chapter 13.2.3:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>The curious <code>= 0</code> syntax was chosen over the obvious alternative of introducing a new keyword <code>pure</code> or <code>abstract</code> because at the time I saw no chance of getting a new keyword accepted. Had I suggested <code>pure</code>, Release 2.0 would have shipped without abstract classes. Given a choice between a nicer syntax and abstract classes, I chose abstract classes. Rather than risking delay and incurring the certain fights over <code>pure</code>, I used the tradition C and C++ convention of using 0 to represent <em>not there</em>.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Anyway looking at the C++ standard (§ 9.2 - Class members) <code>= 0</code> is called <em>pure-specifier</em>.</p>
