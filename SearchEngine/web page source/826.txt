<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I think you're thinking of '<strong>Zeitgeist</strong>'. It typically refers to the general 'spirit' of an era - that is to say 'the culture' - but can include things like art, architecture and fashion that represent an era as they are so closely entwined with the dominant culture (see <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeitgeist">Wikipedia</a>)</p>

<p>noun, German.
1.
the spirit of the time; general trend of thought or feeling characteristic of a particular period of time.</p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>As long as you are specific about what you are referring to, <a href="http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/us/definition/american_english/emblematic">emblematic</a> may be used:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>adj</p>
  
  <p>Serving as a symbol of a particular quality or concept; symbolic</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In the particular sense you are looking for, a common phrase would be that something is "emblematic of its time."</p>

<p>Example:</p>

<p>"This book is emblematic of Russian literature at the turn of the century."</p>

<p>You may be after 'anachronistic' rather than 'dated' as proposed in another answer.</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>The training video they showed us was anachronistic.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In sense 1.1 from <a href="http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/anachronistic" rel="nofollow">Oxford Dictionaries</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>However - as always - it depends on the context of what you are trying to say...</p>
