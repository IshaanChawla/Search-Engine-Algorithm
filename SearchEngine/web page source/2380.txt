        <p style=""> 	<strong style="">Objects</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	The object is the thing that the subject of the </p> <p style=""> 	sentence acts upon. </p> <p style=""> 	I drive a car. </p> <p style=""> 	I eat an apple every day. </p> <p style=""> 	I&#039;m buying a house. </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">Object Suffixes</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? In Hungarian a &#039;t&#039; must be added to the object of </p> <p style=""> 	the sentence. </p> <p style=""> 	You may also need a linking vowel. </p> <p style=""> 	? There are five common endings: </p> <p style=""> 	-t, -at, -ot, -et, -t </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-t (first case)</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Add to words ending in vowels. Lengthen the </p> <p style=""> 	final vowel if it&#039;s short. </p> <p style=""> 	alma (apple) =&gt; almt </p> <p style=""> 	aut (car) =&gt; autt </p> <p style=""> 	vese (kidney) =&gt; vest </p> <p style=""> 	cip? (shoe) =&gt; cip?t </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-t (second case)</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Add to words ending in s, sz or r. </p> <p style=""> 	b?r (skin) =&gt; b?rt </p> <p style=""> 	sr (beer) =&gt; srt </p> <p style=""> 	ks (knife) =&gt; kst </p> <p style=""> 	kolbsz (sausage) =&gt; kolbszt </p> <p style=""> 	hs (meat) =&gt; hst </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-et (first case)</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? After words ending in cs, t, ty, gy, k or m </p> <p style=""> 	kert (garden) =&gt; kertet </p> <p style=""> 	hegy (hill / mountain) =&gt; hegyet </p> <p style=""> 	szk (chair) =&gt; szket </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-et (second case)</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? After words ending in a double consonant and </p> <p style=""> 	containing front vowels. </p> <p style=""> 	meggy (sour cherry) =&gt; meggyet </p> <p style=""> 	tbb (more) =&gt; tbbet </p> <p style=""> 	csekk (bank cheque) =&gt; csekket </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-ot</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Usually added to words with back or mixed </p> <p style=""> 	vowels </p> <p style=""> 	pk (spider) =&gt; pkot </p> <p style=""> 	kabt (coat) =&gt; kabtot </p> <p style=""> 	virg (flower) =&gt; virgot </p> <p style=""> 	vonat (tram) =&gt; vonatot </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-at</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Single-vowel words containing a or  and </p> <p style=""> 	ending in a consonant </p> <p style=""> 	agy (brain) =&gt; agyat </p> <p style=""> 	gy (bed) =&gt; gyat </p> <p style=""> 	mj (liver) =&gt; mjat </p> <p style=""> 	vr (castle) =&gt; vrat </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">-t</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Multiple-vowel words containing front rounded </p> <p style=""> 	vowels (, ?, u?, ?) </p> <p style=""> 	b?rnd (suitcase) =&gt; b?rndt </p> <p style=""> 	knyk (elbow) =&gt; knykt </p> <p style=""> 	f?nk (boss) =&gt; f?nkt </p> <p style=""> 	krm (nail) =&gt; krmt (slightly irregular) </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">Irregular Objects</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	? Many words are irregular, so try to learn the </p> <p style=""> 	object when you learn the word. e.g. fagyi (icecream) </p> <p style=""> 	=&gt; fagyit (i does not get lengthened) </p> <p style=""> 	? If the last vowel is long, often it will get </p> <p style=""> 	shortened: vz (water) =&gt; vizet, kerk (wheel) </p> <p style=""> 	=&gt; kereket, t (road) =&gt; utat </p> <p style=""> 	? Single-vowel words ending in a vowel often get </p> <p style=""> 	a v added and the vowel may be shortened: </p> <p style=""> 	k? (stone) =&gt; kvet, l (horse) - lovat </p> <p style=""> 	<strong style="">Homework</strong> </p> <p style=""> 	Form the objective version of each of the </p> <p style=""> 	following words: </p> <p style=""> 	? vros (town) </p> <p style=""> 	? orszg (country) </p> <p style=""> 	? kincs (treasure) </p> <p style=""> 	? tl (bowl) </p> <p style=""> 	? rm (happiness) </p> <p style=""> 	? fa (tree) </p>    </td>
