<td  id="content" colspan="7"><h1 class="content_heading">Customized Onsite Ajax Training Courses</h1><!-- #BeginLibraryItem "/Library/Quotes.lbi" -->

  Looking for practical, hands-on training on Ajax and RIAs onsite at <i>your</i> organization? Look no further!
  These courses are <i>personally</i> developed and taught by 
  leading Java EE and Ajax developer, speaker, and author 
  <a href="about-instructor.html">Marty Hall</a>. 
  No contract instructor regurgitating someone else's  materials! Marty has taught 
  Ajax, GWT, and Java EE courses onsite for <a href="about-instructor.html#clients">dozens of organizations</a> 
  in the US, Canada, Mexico, Australia, Japan, Puerto Rico, India, and the Philippines,  
  all to <a href="student-reviews.html">rave reviews</a>.
</p>
<p>
  If you have a group of 
  at least eight interested developers (10 for courses outside the US or Canada),
  <a href="mailto:hall@coreservlets.com">contact Marty</a> to arrange a course
  at your location. Onsite courses are easier administratively, 
  are better for clients since the topics and pace can be customized,
  are more cost effective for students since no travel is required,
  and are more convenient (for companies in the Baltimore/Washington area) because
  the schedule is flexible (e.g. afternoons or evenings instead of <i>n</i> consecutive days).
  However, if you have too few developers for an onsite course, check out
  our <a href="http://courses.coreservlets.com/public-courses/ajax/">
    upcoming Ajax training course in Maryland</a> (co-sponsored by the Johns Hopkins
  <i>Engineering for Professionals</i> program). We also have a 
  <a href="http://courses.coreservlets.com/public-courses/oslo-norway/ajax.html">similar Ajax course in Oslo Norway</a>
  in October 2010 (co-sponsored by Bouvet ASA).
</p>
<h2 class="book_part">Main Course Options</h2>
<p>
  There are a variety of types of Ajax courses that we can present at your organization. All courses are in lecture/lab format
  with a heavy emphasis on best practices and pragmatic approaches. <a href="mailto:hall@coreservlets.com">Email the instructor</a> 
  to inquire about a version onsite at <i>your</i> organization.
</p>
<ul>
  <li><b>General Ajax Programming.</b> This course surveys a broad range of Ajax-related topics: foundational
    techniques, JavaScript, development and debugging tools, JSON and XML data handling, AjaxTags, and
    an introduction to several of the most popular Ajax/JavaScript toolkits (Prototype, Scriptaculous,
    jQuery, Dojo, and Ext-JS). GWT can be included or omitted, depending on your
    needs. For most clients, we present this in a 5-day
    lecture/lab course at your organization, but for clients in the Baltimore/Washington area, more flexible schedule options
    are also available. This is the most commonly requested version of our Ajax training.</li>
  <li><b>Ajax Programming with jQuery (or Ext-JS, Prototype/Scriptaculous, Dojo, or Google Closure).</b> 
    This version of the course picks one of the popular JavaScript libraries 
    (jQuery, Ext-JS, Prototype/Scriptaculous, Dojo, or Google Closure), introduces it early in the course, and
    uses it for most of the main Ajax techniques (XML data, JSON data, JSON-RPC, etc.) Neither GWT nor the other JavaScript
    libraries are covered. This option is a good choice if your organization has already settled on one of
    the JavaScript libraries. Depending on your level of JavaScript experience, this can be a 3-5 day course.
    As always, for clients in the Baltimore/Washington area, more flexible schedule options
    are also available. </li>
  <li><b>GWT (Google Web Toolkit) Development.</b> This course covers GWT <i>only</i>: it does
    not look at direct Ajax programming with JavaScript except briefly in the introduction and in the JSNI sections.
    Depending on your Java experience and what topics you want covered, this can be a 2-3 day course.
    As always, for clients in the Baltimore/Washington area, more flexible schedule options
    are also available. For details, see <a href="gwt-training.html">the GWT training course page</a>.</li>
  <li><b>Ajax with JSF.</b> We can also give training on JSF (1.x or 2.0), with a heavy
    emphasis on the Ajax components and libraries. For details on this, <a href="mailto:hall@coreservlets.com">email Marty</a> or see
    the <a href="jsf-training.html">JSF</a> training pages.
    JSF 2.0 has well-integrated Ajax support, and many of the third-party component libraries for JSF 1.<i>x</i>
    also have extensive Ajax support.</li>
  <li><b>A Custom Combination of Topics.</b> We can mix and match topics to fit your needs. For example, since you
    need both server-side code and client-side code to build Ajax apps, a number
