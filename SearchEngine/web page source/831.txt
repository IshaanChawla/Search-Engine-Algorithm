<p>I am going to Santa Monica for 4 weeks to polish my English in July and would like to volunteer at the Planetery Society located in Pasadena.</p>

<p>I am a Swiss citizen and have a biometric passport. However I don't have any special VISA, as there is no VISA needed for the school I am attending and with ESTA I am allowed to stay up to 90 days in the US.</p>

<p>Now is it legally allowed to do volunteer work during my stay?</p>

<td class="answercell">
    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>In general <strong>it is allowed to volunteer in the US</strong> when you are there on a B1 or B2 visa, or on visa waiver program, and for most other visas that do not permit paid employment. However there are some significant restrictions, and it is best to check them out.</p>

<p>The most significant restriction is that <strong>you can only volunteer to do things that are normally done on a voluntary basis</strong>, i.e. without pay. So for example you could volunteer at a hospital as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hospital_volunteer">'candy striper'</a> because that is a volunteer position, and you would be working alongside other unpaid volunteers. You could not do the work of a nurse <em>even if you volunteered to do it unpaid</em> because a nurse is normally a paid position.</p>

<p>You also can't do "anything that would benefit a commercial enterprise", and there are restrictions on things like unpaid internships. However assuming that the Planetary Society of Pasadena is a not-for-profit you should be fine.</p>

<p>Here are some references: <a href="http://www.vfp.org/t-volunteer-in-the-us.aspx">Volunteers for Peace</a>, <a href="http://www.visaservices.duke.edu/volunteer.html">Duke University</a>, <a href="http://www.nolo.com/legal-encyclopedia/getting-us-visa-b-1-do-volunteer-work.html">Nolo</a>, <a href="http://www.fws.gov/volunteers/internantionalVolunteers.html">US Fish and Wildlife service</a></p>
