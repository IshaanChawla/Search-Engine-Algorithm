Students will also be introduced to the use of microeconomic applications to address problems in current economic policy throughout the semester.</p>             <p><strong>Instructor:</strong> Prof. Jonathan Gruber</p></td>         </tr>     </tbody> </table></div></div></div></div> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <p>&nbsp;</p> <h2 class="subhead"><a name="eecs"></a>Electrical Engineering and Computer Science</h2> <div class="courseBox"> <div style="height: 210px;" id="courseInfoInner"><div class="coursePreviewTop"><h3 id="courseTitle">Introduction to Computer Science and Programming</h3></div> <div class="coursePreviewBottom"><div class="images"><img height="90" width="120" src="/courses/electrical-engineering-and-computer-science/6-00sc-introduction-to-computer-science-and-programming-spring-2011/6-00scf11-th.jpg" id="courseThumb" class="first" alt="6-00scf11-th.jpg" />                 <br /> <a href="/courses/electrical-engineering-and-computer-science/6-00sc-introduction-to-computer-science-and-programming-spring-2011" id="viewCourse"><img alt="Click here to view this course." src="/images/button_view-course.png" />                 </a></div> <div class="info"><table class="coursePreviewTable">     <tbody>         <tr>             <td colspan="2"><p class="first"><em>Introduction to Computer Science and Programming</em> is aimed at students with little or no programming experience.
