							<div class="edu_grid" style="width: 690px;"><div style="margin: -15px 0px 0px 0px; padding: 0px;"><div style="float: right; margin: 18px 30px 12px; width: 230px; font-size: 0.8em;"><p style="margin-bottom: 0;">Eric Klopfer, Haynes Miller, and Karen Willcox. &ldquo;<a href="http://web.mit.edu/fnl/volume/265/ocw.html">OCW Educator: Sharing the &ldquo;How&rdquo; as well as the &ldquo;What&rdquo; of MIT Education</a>.&rdquo; <em>MIT Faculty Newsletter</em>, May/June 2014.</p></div> <div style="float: left; margin: 15px 30px 0px; font-size: 1.2em; border-right: 1px solid #931101; width: 370px; position: absolute;"><p style="margin-bottom: 0;"><em>&ldquo;What really goes on behind the scenes in designing a course at MIT? ... What do MIT faculty say about what worked and what didn&rsquo;t?&rdquo;</em></p></div></div> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <p style="margin-bottom: 20px;">OCW Educator is an initiative to share insights on questions like these, to enhance the value of OCW for educators. This page highlights resources developed for OCW Educator, plus other related MIT education and teaching resources.</p> <div class="full alpha omega" style="width: 690px;"><a name="thiscourse"></a> <h2 class="title">This Course at MIT</h2></div> <p><img src="/courses/this-course-at-mit/this-course-list-2col.png" alt="A sample of a This Course at MIT page." style="float: right; margin-left: 26px; margin-bottom: 18px; width: 462px; height: 263px; border: 1px solid #000;" /><a href="/courses/this-course-at-mit">This Course at MIT</a> is a course page that shares information about how a given course was taught at MIT&mdash;what the logistics of the course were and how the course materials published on the OCW site were originally used on campus. We hope that such information will help you better understand and use the course materials.</p> <p>The page typically has multiple sections, including Course Outcomes, Curriculum Information, The Classroom, Assessment, Student Information, How Student Time Was Spent, Instructor Insights, and Course Team Roles.</p> <p>Some of these pages feature extensive commentary from MIT instructors on their experiences, ideas, and approaches as educators. These pages include:</p> <div style="float: left; margin-left: 15px; width: 295px;"><ul>     <li><a href="/courses/architecture/4-241j-theory-of-city-form-spring-2013/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>4.241J/11.330J Theory of City Form</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/biology/7-013-introductory-biology-spring-2013/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>7.013 Introductory Biology</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/physics/8-421-atomic-and-optical-physics-i-spring-2014/this-course-at-mit#insights"><em>8.421 Atomic and Optical Physics I</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/brain-and-cognitive-sciences/9-00sc-introduction-to-psychology-fall-2011/this-course-at-mit#insights"><em>9.00SC Introduction to Psychology</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/urban-studies-and-planning/11-124-introduction-to-education-looking-forward-and-looking-back-on-education-fall-2011/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>11.124 Introduction to Education: Looking Forward and Looking Back on Education</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/earth-atmospheric-and-planetary-sciences/12-001-introduction-to-geology-fall-2013/this-course-at-mit#insights"><em>12.001 Introduction to Geology</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/sloan-school-of-management/15-279-management-communication-for-undergraduates-fall-2012/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>15.279 Management Communication for Undergraduates</em></a></li> </ul></div> <div style="float: left; margin-left: 40px; width: 295px;"><ul>     <li><a href="/courses/sloan-school-of-management/15-s07-globalhealth-lab-spring-2013/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>15.S07 GlobalHealth Lab</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/aeronautics-and-astronautics/16-06-principles-of-automatic-control-fall-2012/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>16.06 Principles of Automatic Control</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/mathematics/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2014/this-course-at-mit#insights"><em>18.05 Introduction to Probability and Statistics</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/mathematics/18-904-seminar-in-topology-spring-2011/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>18.904 Seminar in Topology</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/history/21h-134j-medieval-economic-history-in-comparative-perspective-spring-2012/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>21H.134J/14.70J Medieval Economic History in Comparative Perspective</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/literature/21l-460-medieval-literature-legends-of-arthur-fall-2013/this-course-at-mit#insights"><em>21L.460 Medieval Literature: Legends of Arthur</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/nuclear-engineering/22-033-nuclear-systems-design-project-fall-2011/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>22.033 Nuclear Systems Design Project</em></a></li>     <li><a href="/courses/experimental-study-group/es-s41-speak-italian-with-your-mouth-full-spring-2012/this-course-at-mit/#insights"><em>ES.S41 Speak Italian With Your Mouth Full</em></a></li> </ul></div> <div style="width: 0; height: 0;" class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <p>We hope the Instructor Insights section will contribute to educators&rsquo; reflections on their own practice and discussions on pedagogical issues.</p> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <div class="full alpha omega" style="width: 690px;"><a name="foreducators"></a> <h2 class="title">&quot;For Educators&quot; Course Publications Emphasizing Pedagogy</h2></div> <p><img width="240" height="180" src="/educator/educator_white-board.jpg" alt="Two students working on a white board." style="float: left; margin-right: 26px;" />Certain courses taught at MIT do not lend themselves very well to traditional OCW publication. These are mainly experiential courses that offer students the opportunity to do research or create a project. Consequently, they may not have some of the basic components typically published on the OCW site, such as a syllabus and lecture notes.</p> <p>OCW Educator has provided a way to represent these courses on the OCW site and give educators a chance to see how they work. These course publications contain extensive description and commentary on the course&rsquo;s structure and pedagogy. With the aid of videos and photographs, they try to convey both the challenges of teaching such courses and the experience of taking them.</p> <p>A premiere example is <a href="/courses/mathematics/18-821-project-laboratory-in-mathematics-spring-2013"><em>18.821 Project Laboratory in Mathematics</em></a>, a course that is designed to give students a sense of what it is like to do mathematical research.</p><p>You might also be interested in <em><a href="/courses/mathematics/18-915-graduate-topology-seminar-kan-seminar-fall-2014">18.915 Graduate Topology Seminar</a></em>, which shows how math students discover the &ldquo;narrative arc&rdquo; shaping the development of Algebraic Topology by leading lectures and engaging in one-on-one conversations with their instructor.</p> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <div class="full alpha omega" style="width: 690px;"><a name="teaching"></a> <h2 class="title">Courses about Teaching and Education</h2></div> <p>Among OCW's many course publications, there is a rich and varied selection of courses about teaching and education. These range from courses about educational technology, to courses about education policy, to an undergraduate sequence that leads to teacher certification. These courses can be located via the <a href="/courses/find-by-number/">OCW Course Finder</a> under the topic <a href="/courses/find-by-topic/#cat=teachingandeducation">Teaching and Education</a>.</p> <p>Some OCW courses include a short introduction video in which faculty summarize the objectives, salient features, and pedagogy for their course. We&rsquo;ve collected a page of <a href="/educator/pedagogy-highlights">Pedagogy Highlights in Course Introduction Videos</a> to feature those videos of particular interest to educators.</p> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <div class="full alpha omega" style="width: 690px;"><a name="usingmaterials"></a> <h2 class="title">Using OCW Course Materials</h2></div> <p>A central goal of OCW is to provide a repository of MIT's core materials for classroom instruction, so that educators around the world can download, modify, and use these materials. We strongly encourage you to explore and use the course materials on OCW in your own teaching.</p> <ul>     <li><a href="/help/get-started-with-ocw">Get started with OCW</a>.</li>     <li><a href="/help/finding-courses">Find relevant courses</a>.</li>     <li>Learn about our <a href="/terms/#cc">license terms</a> and <a href="/help/faq-intellectual-property">how you can use the materials published on OCW</a>.</li>     <li>Read <a href="/about/ocw-stories#educators">current</a> and <a href="/about/ocw-stories/ocw-stories-archive#educators">archived</a> stories about how educators have used OCW materials.</li>     <li>Learn about some of the <a href="/faculty">MIT faculty and instructors whose course materials are shared on MIT OpenCourseWare</a>.</li> </ul> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div> <div class="full alpha omega" style="width: 690px;"><a name="related"></a> <h2 class="title">Related Resources</h2></div> <p>Resources about education at MIT:</p> <ul>     <li><a href="http://web.mit.edu/aboutmit/"> About MIT</a></li>     <li><a href="http://mitadmissions.org/index.php/"> MIT Admissions</a></li>     <li><a href="http://web.mit.edu/firstyear/"> The First Year at MIT</a></li>     <li><a href="http://web.mit.edu/catalog/index.html"> MIT Course Catalog Bulletin</a></li>     <li><a href="http://student.mit.edu/catalog/index.cgi"> MIT Subject Listing and Schedule</a></li>     <li><a href="http://web.mit.edu/urop/"> MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program</a></li>     <li><a href="https://due.mit.edu/"> MIT Office of the Dean for Undergraduate Education</a></li>     <li><a href="http://odge.mit.edu/"> MIT Office of the Dean for Graduate Education</a></li> </ul> <p>Resources related to teaching at MIT:</p> <ul>     <li><a href="http://teachingexcellence.mit.edu/"> Teaching Excellence at MIT</a></li>     <li><a href="http://tll.mit.edu/"> Teaching &amp; Learning Laboratory</a></li> </ul> <div class="clear">&nbsp;</div></div><p>&nbsp;</p>
