		<h1 class="heading" itemprop="headline"><em>Duke Nukem</em> lawsuit ends with settlement, possible license transfer</h1>
		<h2 class="standalone-deck" itemprop="description">Screenshot confirms that embroiled <em>Mass Destruction</em> game reborn as <em>Bombshell</em>.</h2>
    		<div class="post-meta">

		<div itemprop="articleBody" class="article-content clearfix">
    
<figure class="intro-image image center full-width" style="width:640px">
      <img src="http://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-18-at-6.55.06-PM-640x476.png" width="640" height="476">
  
    <figcaption class="caption">
			<div class="caption-text">This image of <em>Duke Nukem: Mass Destruction</em>, which leaked along with court documents last week, contains nearly identical assets as upcoming 3D Realms game <em>Bombshell</em>.</div>
	
			<div class="caption-credit">
							<a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.scribd.com/doc/264520063/Exhibit-s-3-Apogee-Software-Ltd-and-Interceptor-Entertainment-ApS">Scribd</a>
				</div>
	  </figcaption>
  </figure>




    <p>A year-long lawsuit between the major players in the <em>Duke Nukem</em> game franchise appears to have reached its conclusion last week, as evidenced by <a href="http://www.neogaf.com/forum/showthread.php?p=164164162#post164164162">a court document leak</a> pointing to a settlement.</p>
<p><em>Duke Nukem Forever</em> publisher Gearbox Software <a href="http://arstechnica.com/gaming/2014/02/gearbox-suing-3d-realms-over-duke-nukem-mass-destruction-rpg/">filed an initial lawsuit in February 2014</a> against 3D Realms, the <em>DN</em> series' original handlers, and parent company Interceptor Entertainment. The suit alleged that Interceptor had breached the <em>Duke Nukem</em> IP handover contract—the one created when <em>Duke Nukem Forever</em> finally <a href="http://arstechnica.com/gaming/2010/09/duke-nukem-forever-is-back-coming-to-both-consoles-and-pc/">inched its way</a> toward its <a href="http://arstechnica.com/gaming/2011/06/duke-nukem-forever-review-barely-playable-unfunny-and-rampantly-offensive/">disastrous</a> launch—when Interceptor later announced a game called <em>Duke Nukem: Mass Destruction</em>.</p>
<p>That contract, conveniently enough, <a href="https://www.scribd.com/doc/264460584/Exhibit-s-1-Asset-Purchase-Agreement">leaked last week</a> on document site Scribd, as did Gearbox's lawsuit allegations. Gearbox's lawyers <a href="https://www.scribd.com/doc/264520063/Exhibit-s-3-Apogee-Software-Ltd-and-Interceptor-Entertainment-ApS#">alleged</a> that 3D Realms heads George Broussard and Scott Miller didn't just infringe on the <em>DNF</em> handover contract, but that they'd "directly confirmed" that 3D Realms had infringed upon "Gearbox's rights to the Duke IP." Though none of 3D Realms' direct statements leaked, the terms of the original contract indicated that the original Duke IP owners still had the rights to work on re-releases of prior games, along with a tentatively titled <em>Duke Nukem: Survivor</em> game.</p>
<p>The leaked documents didn't clarify whether 3D Realms had ever asserted that the <em>Mass Destruction</em> title was protected in the contract, due to possibly being the same game as <em>Survivor</em>, but Ars Technica can confirm that the <em>Mass Destruction</em> screenshots included in Gearbox's filing perfectly match one of the levels in upcoming 3D Realms game <em>Bombshell</em>, a third-person gun-action game that, not surprisingly, draws a lot of inspiration from the <em>Duke</em> games.</p>
<p>Though the settlement leak, dated May 8, didn't hint to any terms, the apparent leaker—NeoGAF forum member Mr. Tibbs—also posted <a href="http://i.imgur.com/wmT7zEY.png">a leaked image</a> of the 2010 contract's "buy-back of Duke IP" terms, thereby hinting to a transfer of the full <em>Duke</em> IP back to 3D Realms from Gearbox Software. When asked about the lawsuit and settlement, a 3D Realms representative declined comment, instead simply stating, "we're super excited for <em>Bombshell</em> to release this summer." We have asked for comment from Gearbox Software, and we will update this post with any response.</p>
