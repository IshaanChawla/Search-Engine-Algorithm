<p>This should be a relatively simple question—one that I cannot seem to find anywhere.</p>

<p>Does, for example, a dog sit <strong><em>down</em></strong> on its haunches, or sit <strong><em>up</em></strong> on its haunches?</p>

<blockquote>
  <p><em>"Charlie sat <strong>up</strong> on his haunches and tilted his head in confusion at the strange command."</em></p>
</blockquote>

<p> </p>

<blockquote>
  <p><em>"Charlie sat <strong>down</strong> on his haunches and tilted his head in confusion at the strange command."</em></p>
</blockquote>

<p>Which one makes more sense? Or is it a universal term?</p>

    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Not having a dog with me to experiment with, I would guess that the anatomical position is identical, but that the preposition reflects the direction of movement. He would sit down on his haunches from a standing position, but sit up on them from a lying position. </p>

<p>Either way, it results in 'sitting on his haunches':</p>

<p><img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/LUEWu.jpg" alt="dog sitting"></p>

<blockquote>
  <p>The big blackie then dropped to all fours and turned to walk away, but changing his mind in midstride, he swung his head about to <a href="https://books.google.com/books?id=_Rx-qbpsz4YC&amp;pg=PA113&amp;lpg=PA113&amp;dq=%22sit+down+on+his+haunches%22&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=5vAsMeO6au&amp;sig=eIXARH1khABEqa7C-8igKSWvZKg&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=VtBcVYHFJcaHsQTEooDwAQ&amp;ved=0CB4Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&amp;q=%22sit%20down%20on%20his%20haunches%22&amp;f=false" rel="nofollow">sit down on his haunches</a> so he could stare at me some more.</p>
  
  <p>When she stopped, <a href="https://books.google.com/books?id=HllFAAAAYAAJ&amp;pg=PA181&amp;lpg=PA181&amp;dq=When+she+stopped,+he+sat+up+on+his+haunches+and+offered+her+his+paw,+then+rolled+over,+then+played+dead+dog,+and+then+began+it+all+over+again,+by+sitting+up+on%22&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=bwyogHpwoS&amp;sig=LvMCDXPclxqqWnxO3M_GutdgPRI&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=gs9cVcPQNMyoNsjIgZAK&amp;ved=0CB4Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&amp;q=When%20she%20stopped%2C%20he%20sat%20up%20on%20his%20haunches%20and%20offered%20her%20his%20paw%2C%20then%20rolled%20over%2C%20then%20played%20dead%20dog%2C%20and%20then%20began%20it%20all%20over%20again%2C%20by%20sitting%20up%20on%22&amp;f=false" rel="nofollow">he sat up on his haunches</a> and offered her his paw, then rolled over, then played dead dog, and then began it all over again, by sitting up on his haunches and offering her his paw... </p>
</blockquote>
