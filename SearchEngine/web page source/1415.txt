<h2 class="global">MIT OpenCourseWare's First 10 Years: 100 Million Served</h2>
<p><strong>On April 4, 2001</strong>, MIT announced it would publish educational materials from all of its courses freely and openly on the Internet. Ten years later, OCW has shared materials from more than 2000 courses with an estimated 100 million individuals worldwide. Join us in celebrating the 10th anniversary of this groundbreaking effort.</p>
<ul class="chevron">
    <li><a href="http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9E07E0DD163EF937A35757C0A9679C8B63&amp;sec=technology&amp;spon=&amp;partner=permalink&amp;exprod=permalink">Read the 2001 announcement</a> in the <em>New York Times</em>.</li>
    <li><a href="http://techtv.mit.edu/collections/fromthevault/videos/11721-t9078-ready-mit-opencourseware-press-conference---april-4-2001">Watch the 2001 MIT OpenCourseWare press conference</a></li>
    <li><a href="/about/next-decade/news">Read news and reflections on 10 years of OpenCourseWare</a></li>
</ul>
<hr style="padding-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 15px; padding-left: 0pt; padding-right: 0pt; margin-bottom: 15px; border-top: rgb(136,136,136) 1px solid; padding-top: 0pt" />
<h2 class="global">OCW's Next Decade: Reaching One Billion Minds</h2>
<p>MIT's goal for the next decade is to increase our reach ten-fold: to reach a billion minds. We aspire by 2021 to make open educational resources like MIT OpenCourseWare the tools to bridge the global gap between human potential and opportunity, so that motivated people everywhere can improve their lives and change the world.</p>

</ul>
