    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">

<p>Through the <a href="http://en.memory-alpha.org/wiki/All_Good_Things..._%28episode%29">final episode of <em>Star Trek: The Next Generation</em></a>, we learn that</p>

<blockquote class="spoiler">
  <p> Picard has created an anomaly in the Devron system in the future. That anomaly got bigger and bigger as it moved back in time and eventually has caused the destruction of human kind in the very, very distant past, because it caused life on Earth to actually not be born.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>This part is really unclear for me. How could the anomaly stop the combination of amino acids into the first proteins? Is it explained in the episode and I just missed that? Beside getting larger and larger, the anomaly itself doesn't seem to have any actual influence on anything through all three (actually four -- including that very, very old Paris) timelines presented in this episode.</p>

    <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>It is sort-of explained, yes. <a href="http://en.memory-alpha.org/wiki/All_Good_Things..._%28episode%29">From your link</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>Data then offers a partial explanation. The anomaly is a multiphasic
  temporal convergence in the space-time continuum, an eruption of
  anti-time. The relationship between time and anti-time is analogous to
  the relationship between matter and anti-matter, and upon contact, the
  two would annihilate each other, causing the rupture in space.</p>
  
  <p>[...]</p>
  
  <p>Q transfers them back to Earth, about 3.5 billion years in the past.
  He shows Picard the sky, which is full of the anomaly. Picard then
  realizes that the anomaly is expanding as it moves back in time. Q
  then shows Picard a pool of green sludge, a pool of amino acids. They
  are about to combine to form the first proteins, however, the
  disturbance from the anomaly stops the combination as it happens. Life
  on Earth doesn't start, and the anomaly stopped the creation of
  Humanity.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The event <a href="http://www.st-minutiae.com/academy/literature329/277.txt">isn't really explained</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>They look into the water.</p>
  
  <p><strong>Q (continuing):</strong> Here they go... the amino acids are moving closer...
  closer... closer... <em>(reacts)</em> Ohhhh! Nothing happened! <em>(to Picard)</em> You
  see what you've done?</p>
  
  <p><em>Picard thinks for a moment, then looks up at the sky.</em></p>
  
  <p><strong>PICARD:</strong>  You mean I caused the anomaly... and the anomaly... in some
  way... disrupted the beginning of life on Earth.</p>
  
  <p><em>Q's eyes bore into Picard.</em></p>
  
  <p><strong>Q:</strong>  Congratulations.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Earlier in the episode a pregnant crew member also lost her baby due to the effects of the anomaly, which Crusher explained like this:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>I think it's the same thing that happened to Geordi. Somehow, the
  temporal energy from the anomaly caused the fetal tissue to <strong>revert to
  an earlier stage of development</strong>. It was as if the unborn child began
  to... grow younger... until finally <strong>the DNA itself began to break
  down</strong>.</p>
</blockquote>

  <p>However, soon afterward, La Forge experiences some pain, and trouble with his VISOR. Data calls sickbay, and Crusher determines that La Forge is growing new eyes. Nurse Ogawa reports that they have reports from two crew members stating that old scars are healing themselves.</p>

<p>And, later:</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>In the present, Picard accidentally bumps into a crew member on his way to sickbay. Inside sickbay, several time-reversing phenomena are occurring. La Forge's visual acuity is improving by the minute, and Alyssa Ogawa lost her baby. Picard enters, and Crusher explains that the baby somehow grew backwards, until the DNA itself broke down. However, the temporal energy seems to be affecting the entire crew, causing everyone to "grow backward," and it could kill all of them.</p>
