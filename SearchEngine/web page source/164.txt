		<h1 class="heading" itemprop="headline">With <em>Crysis Analogue Edition</em>, you can finally say “yes, it runs Crysis”</h1>
		<h2 class="standalone-deck" itemprop="description">Crysis Analogue Edition turns graphical shooter into eight-player strategy game.</h2>
    		<div class="post-meta">
      
  <p class="byline" itemprop="author creator" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

	<section id="article-guts">
		<div itemprop="articleBody" class="article-content clearfix">
    
<figure class="intro-image image center full-width" style="width:640px">
      <img src="http://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/photo-original-640x480.png" width="640" height="480">
  
  </figure>




    <p>For years PC hardware enthusiasts the world over puzzled over motherboards, CPUs, RAM, and whether it was better to go with an SLI or Crossfire setup, just to answer that age old question: "Can it play Crysis?" Turns out, you'll soon be able to answer that question without resorting to spending thousands of dollars on hardcore pixel-pushing hardware.</p>
<p>Yes, of all the things that might lend themselves well to being recreated in board game form, Crytek's first-person graphical showpony <em>Crysis</em> isn't the first that springs to mind. But German company Frame6 (with the blessing of Crytek) is skirting around the series' sometimes flimsy gameplay to bring <em>Crysis Analogue Edition - The Board Game</em> to life—or at least try to, by <a href="https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/frame6/crysis-analogue-edition-the-board-game/description">asking for €85,000 over on Kickstarter</a>.</p>
<p>So, how does a first-person shooter translate into board game? <em>Crysis Analogue Edition</em> turns the shooter into an eight-player strategy game with two types of game: Instant Action and Capture the Relay. Instant Action is a form of team deathmatch, with every frag scoring one point. Capture the Relay is a little more involved, but the basic premise is that you need to nab a relay token from the board and take it to an extraction point, with the opposing team doing the same.</p>
<p>It isn't the most original board game ever, but if you're into the whole <em>Crysis</em> thing, the extras are pretty neat. Those include 165 playing cards complete with classic <em>Crysis</em> weapons like the SCAR rifle and Predator Bow, supply crate and wall cover tokens, and of course a nicely moulded selection of CELL and USF soldiers.</p>
<figure class="video" style="width:100%"><iframe style="display:block" type="text/html" width="100%" height="360" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/SiU-eRENc54?start=0&wmode=transparent" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><figcaption class="caption"><div class="caption-text">Crysis Analogue Edition</div> </figcaption></figure>
<p>If you fancy it, <em>Crysis Analogue Edition</em> is going for €70 (~$80) over Frame6's Kickstarter page, although the project is still some ways off hitting its €85,000 target.</p>
<p>While the whole idea of translating an action-based shooter to board game might seem strange, <em>Crysis Analogue Edition</em> certainly isn't the first. Third-person bro-fest <em>Gears of War</em> has already been <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Fantasy-Flight-Games-GE01-Gears/dp/1616611693/?tag=georiot-grd-21">translated into board game form</a>, as well as <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Fantasy-Flight-Games-VA08-Doom/dp/1589941810/?tag=georiot-grd-21">seminal shooter <em>Doom</em></a>. <em>Resident Evil</em>, <em><a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Fantasy-Flight-Games-Starcraft-Edition/dp/B004VNMN1Q?tag=georiot-grd-21&amp;SubscriptionId=AKIAI3FTSIOZQ7GOWL4Q&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;camp=2025&amp;creative=165953&amp;creativeASIN=B004VNMN1Q">Starcraft</a></em>, and yes, even <em><a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Street-Fighter-Deck-Building-Game/dp/B00CRUIJI6/?tag=georiot-grd-21">Street Fighter</a></em> have also been given the board game treatment.</p>
