		<h2 class="standalone-deck" itemprop="description">Microsoft brings its revamped suite of office apps to Android phones. </h2>

		<div itemprop="articleBody" class="article-content clearfix">
    
<figure class="intro-image image center full-width" style="width:640px">
      <img src="http://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Office-for-Android-phone-Preview-now-available-1-1-640x358.png" width="640" height="358">
  
    <figcaption class="caption">
			<div class="caption-text">The new version of Microsoft Office on an Android phone.</div>
	
			<div class="caption-credit">
							<a rel="nofollow" href="http://blogs.office.com/2015/05/19/office-for-android-phone-preview-now-available/">Microsoft</a>
				</div>
	  </figcaption>
  </figure>




    <p>A few months after the launch of <a href="http://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2015/01/office-for-android-tablets-is-here-just-mind-the-system-requirements/">Office for Android tablets</a>, Microsoft has announced a preview of "Office for Android phone"—a shrunken down version of the tablet app. The preview will eventually replace the existing <a href="http://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2013/07/hands-on-with-microsofts-office-mobile-for-android/">Office Mobile</a>.</p>
<p>For the most part, it looks like the Android tablet version. There's finger-friendly versions of Word, Excel, and PowerPoint that let you create, open, and edit office documents. Best of all, the suite is free!</p>
<p>Saving is all done in the cloud, and the announcement notes that "OneDrive, OneDrive for Business, SharePoint, Dropbox, Google Drive, and Box" are all available.</p>
<p>Since the release is just a preview, Microsoft is using the built-in Android beta system. Signing up for the preview means heading over to <a href="https://plus.google.com/communities/115302484554583402046">Microsoft's Google+ community</a> (what strange times we live in) and opting into the preview app.</p>
